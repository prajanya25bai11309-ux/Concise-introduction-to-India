Problem Statement
So much info. Everywhere.
Blogs, reels. Maps, suggestions. Most fake or recycled.
Tourists get confused. It mess up their mind. Decision fatigue every step.
One site says one thing. Another says opposite. Who to trust?
Want to see mountains? End up lost in ads. Clickbait every place.
Reliable local insights? Hard to find.
Too much. Too scattered. Overload.​

Scope of the Project
Make travel simple.
One-stop. Stories first, facts second.
No fluff, no clickbait.
If it’s temple, tell tale. If it’s fort, say history. Give moral too.
Tourists pick states in menu. Journey is recursion. Real info, not spam.
Show what matters. Hide the noise.
Project covers all states—folk, historic places, must-see.
Easy to use. No dependencies required.

Target Users
Tourists lost in the web.
Solo wanderers, families.
People want real advice. Not ads.
Foreigners, students, researchers.
Anyone who tired from too much info. Wants story, wants truth.

High-Level Features
Abrupt menu. Pick your states.
Get folk tale, places, history.
Typewriter effect. Slow prints, feels like old book.
Random or full journey modes.
Recursion—the journey is an adventure, not just code stuff.
No login, no nonsense.
Works offline.
Guide, not just data. Stories. Every time.
